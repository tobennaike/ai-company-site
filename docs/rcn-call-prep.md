# RCN Call Prep — Auxo Partners Partnership
**Caller:** Tobenna Ikejiani, Founder — Auxo Partners
**Date:** March 2026
**Format:** Warm intro / family relationship — keep it conversational, not salesy

---

## 1. Call Objective

Get RCN CPAs to commit to a pilot partnership: identify 3-5 existing clients willing to test Auxo's AI tools at no cost, in exchange for a 15-20% revenue share on any client who converts to a paid engagement.

---

## 2. Opening Pitch
*(Say this verbatim or close to it)*

> "I wanted to loop you in on something I've been building — Auxo Partners. The short version: we build AI-powered systems for small businesses — automating their sales follow-up, invoicing, customer support, financial reporting. We're targeting firms in the $1-10M range, and I think RCN's client base is a perfect fit.
>
> What I'm proposing is a partnership where we run free pilots for 3-5 of your clients. You take zero risk — we do all the work. If they convert to paying clients, RCN earns 15-20% of that revenue as a referral fee, ongoing. I just want to walk you through what that could look like."

---

## 3. Key Talking Points

- **The AI moment is now.** SMBs are getting left behind while enterprise companies automate everything. RCN's clients are sitting on inefficiencies that cost them real money — Auxo closes that gap.

- **Four core products, immediate ROI.** AI Sales Funnel (never miss a lead), Invoice/Document Processor (eliminate manual entry), AI Customer Support Chatbot (24/7 coverage without headcount), and Financial Reporting Automation (already live via Derra AI). Each one solves a problem RCN's clients already complain about.

- **Pilots are zero-risk for RCN and the clients.** No cost, no contract commitment during the pilot. We come in, implement, show results. The client decides if they want to keep it.

- **Revenue share is real upside.** One converted client at $3K/mo = $450-$600/mo back to RCN. Five clients = $2,250-$3,000/mo in passive referral income with no additional work on RCN's end.

- **Auxo handles everything.** RCN's only role is the warm intro. We scope, implement, train, and support. The firm isn't taking on any service obligation.

- **We already have a working product.** Derra AI (financial reporting automation) is already deployed. We're not pitching vaporware — we can demo it today.

- **RCN gets first-mover positioning.** Being the firm that introduced AI to their clients before competitors did is a differentiator. It deepens client relationships and increases stickiness.

---

## 4. Anticipated Objections + Responses

**"Our clients aren't tech-savvy / won't be interested."**
> "That's exactly who this is built for. We don't hand clients a tool and walk away — we implement it, train their team, and hold their hand through adoption. The business owner doesn't need to be technical at all. We've designed this for the same type of operator who uses QuickBooks because someone set it up for them."

---

**"We don't want to recommend something unproven to our clients."**
> "Totally fair — that's why we're proposing pilots, not sales. There's no ask on the client until they've seen results. If the pilot doesn't deliver, they walk away with nothing lost. RCN's reputation stays protected, and we've actually added value to those client relationships regardless of outcome."

---

**"What's the time commitment for RCN?"**
> "Minimal. We're asking for warm intros — an email or a quick mention on a client call. That's it. Auxo handles the rest: scheduling, scoping, implementation, follow-up. We may loop you in for a 20-minute intro call per client, but beyond that, we run it."

---

**"15-20% — how does that actually get tracked and paid?"**
> "Simple. Any client RCN refers who signs a paid contract — we tag them as RCN-sourced. Monthly invoices go to the client, and we cut a referral payment to RCN within 30 days. We'll put this in a simple one-page partnership agreement so it's clean and documented. No ambiguity."

---

## 5. The Ask / Close
*(Say this exactly when the conversation is ready to close)*

> "Here's what I'd like to walk away with from this call: your commitment to pilot this with 3 to 5 clients, and a short list of who you think would be the best fit to start with. It doesn't need to be a final decision today — but if you're open to it, I'd love to send over a one-page overview you can review, and we can reconnect in a few days to confirm the names and kick off the first intro. Does that work?"
